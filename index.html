About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Laboratoire Interdisciplinaire des Sciences du Numérique (LISN), CNRS, Université Paris-Saclay within the LaHDAK team. I hold a Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Nairobi.

My academic career is defined by a dual focus on teaching and advanced research. I currently serve as a Teaching Assistant at CentraleSupélec (Université Paris-Saclay) and a Part-time Lecturer at the University of Nairobi (Department of Physics). I also served as a lecturer for the EASY-ML School, training the next generation of African researchers in machine learning.

My research expertise lies in Scientific Computing and AI-driven Physics. From developing chemometric models for food safety to integrating heterogeneous graph data for the LHC at CERN, I build reproducible, high-performance computational tools.
